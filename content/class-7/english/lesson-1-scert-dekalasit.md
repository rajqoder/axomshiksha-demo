## ACTIVITIES

### 1. Let’s find out how much we have understood:

**(a) What is a hobby?**  
**Ans:** A hobby is an activity that a person enjoys doing in his/her free time for pleasure and relaxation.

**(b) What do hobbies give us?**  
**Ans:** Hobbies give us fun, joy, relaxation, and help us use our free time fruitfully.

**(c) What is philately? What difficulty would you face if you take up philately?**  
**Ans:** Philately is the collecting and study of stamps.  
If we take up philately, we may face difficulty in getting old and rare stamps.

**(d) What is Irfan’s hobby? What does he like to do?**  
**Ans:** Irfan’s hobby is singing. He likes to sing, listen to classical music, and play tunes on his harmonium.

**(e) Name a few indoor and outdoor hobbies.**  
**Ans:** Indoor hobbies: singing, painting, reading, collecting stamps.  
Outdoor hobbies: gardening, swimming, trekking, photography.

**(f) What is a kitchen garden?**    
**Ans:** A kitchen garden is a small garden near the house where vegetables are grown for daily use.

**(g) “Every morning I water the plants and clean the beds.” – What does ‘beds’ mean here?**  
**Ans:** Here, ‘beds’ means the prepared soil areas in the garden where plants are grown.

### 7. Read this sentence. Note the part of the sentence in italics.

A happy person is usually *as busy as a bee.*  

The part of the sentence in italics is called an **idiom**. It means **“very busy”**.

The use of common idioms makes English appear more natural. Some common English idioms are:  
- as busy as a bee  
- bag and baggage  
- beat about the bush

**Try to use these idioms in sentences of your own. You can look up the dictionary to learn their meaning.**
